My Story

Where It All Started

It started at NextShark, where I helped transform a scrappy financial news site into a cultural powerhouse for Asian American voices. We were a small team with big ambition, and I led the charge in growing our audience organically—reaching over 20 million people a month and turning Instagram into a hub for 150,000+ monthly engagements. That experience taught me how to move fast, ride trends, and build loyal communities from the ground up.

Merging Creativity and Partnerships

At Pollen, I stepped into a hybrid creative-strategic role, designing social content while brokering high-impact partnerships—like a $250K deal with Bumble. It was fast-paced, experimental, and a crash course in what it means to blend culture with commerce.

Marketing at Scale with Global Brands

That balance of creativity and structure came into sharper focus at Hyatt Hotels, where I managed marketing efforts for three hotels under two global brands. I built campaign calendars around major events like the Long Beach Grand Prix, maintained brand consistency across channels, and advised internal teams on best practices. I learned how to operate within brand systems—without losing the human voice behind the content.

Shaping Community in Real Time

Then came Monster Energy, where I took on the digital “eyes and ears” of the brand. I developed campaign strategies that bridged live events and online presence, engaging fans in real time while protecting brand reputation. It was here that I really honed my skills in community management—knowing when to amplify, when to listen, and how to keep things authentic at scale.

Driving Growth with Purpose

Now at Niagara Bottling, I’ve brought that full toolkit to a brand ready to grow. In under a year, I’ve led social strategy that more than doubled our audience and increased engagement by 1500%, all while cutting ad spend in half. I manage our external agency with a tight grip on brand consistency and a wide lens for creative opportunities. We’re not just selling water—we’re building a brand people actually want to follow.
